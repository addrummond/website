I'm a postdoc in the Department of Philosophy at Durham University, working on the Un-Cartesian Linguistics project with Wolfram Hinzen.

My research focuses on the division of labor between the narrow syntax and the interfaces. So far, I've focused on binding and scope phenomena. In both domains, I think that the role of the interpretative interfaces has been somewhat overestimated, and the role of the PF interface underestimated.

I defended my dissertation in July 2011 at UMD. My advisor was Norbert Hornstein.

I wrote the Ibex software for performing certain kinds of behavioral psycholinguistic experiments online.
